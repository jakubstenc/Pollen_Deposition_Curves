Methodological Note: Data Conversion for Glechoma longituba

Source: Zhang et al. (2011), Nordic Journal of Botany.

Data Transformation: The original time-series data for Glechoma longituba (Figure 1) presented pollen dynamics as relative percentages. To ensure consistency with the absolute values used elsewhere in this dataset, these percentages were converted to absolute pollen grain counts using baseline biological data explicitly stated in the article text:

Pollen Removal: Cumulative removal percentages were converted to absolute grains based on the reported mean total pollen production per flower (Mean = 24,166 grains).

Pollen Deposition: Deposition percentages were converted to absolute grains based on the reported mean maximum stigmatic pollen load observed at the end of the female phase (Mean = 389 grains).

Assumption: For pollen deposition, it is assumed that the ‚Äú100%‚Äù scale on the figure‚Äôs secondary y-axis represents the physiological saturation point of the stigma (approx. 389 grains) rather than the total pollen production of the flower, as the latter would imply biologically unrealistic deposition rates for this species.
